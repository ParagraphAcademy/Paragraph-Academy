IELTS (Academic & General) — quick facts

IELTS tests Listening, Reading, Writing and Speaking. Academic is for university admission; General Training is for migration and work. Test takes ~2 hours 45 minutes; the Speaking test may be on a different day.

Format

  • Listening — 30 minutes
  • Reading — 60 minutes
  • Writing — 60 minutes (Task 1 + Task 2)
  • Speaking — 11–14 minutes

Scoring

Band 0–9 per skill; overall band average rounded to 0.5.

Prep plan (8 weeks)

  1. Week 1: Full diagnostic test
  2. Weeks 2–3: Listening & Reading intensive
  3. Weeks 4–5: Writing practice + feedback
  4. Weeks 6–7: Speaking mocks, fluency & pronunciation
  5. Week 8: Full mock tests and review

Writing tips

  • Task 2 has more weight — spend ~40 minutes
  • Plan (5–7 min), write, check for coherence and grammar
  • Use paragraphs with clear topic sentences

Speaking checklist

  • Practice cue cards for 1–2 minutes
  • Extend answers with reasons and examples
  • Record and review for fillers and repetition
Sample IELTS Writing Task 2:

Some people say advertising encourages consumption. To what extent do you agree?

Sample Speaking cue card:

Describe a memorable trip you took. You should say where you went, who you went with, what you did, and why it was memorable.

PTE Academic — quick facts

PTE Academic is a computer-based English test that assesses speaking, writing, reading and listening using integrated tasks. Faster results (often within 5 business days) and fully automated scoring for most items.

Format

  • Test length: ~2 hours
  • Sections are integrated — e.g., "listen and write summary", "read aloud"
  • Scored on a scale; reported as overall and communicative skills

Prep plan (6–8 weeks)

  1. Week 1: Diagnostic & familiarisation with item types
  2. Weeks 2–4: Skills practice — integrated tasks
  3. Weeks 5–6: Timed full tests and review

PTE tips

  • Learn task instructions — they are strict and time-limited
  • For "read aloud" and "repeat sentence", clarity and pronunciation matter
  • Manage typing speed for "write essay" and "summarise written text"

Scoring note

PTE uses automated scoring algorithms; pronunciation, content, and grammar all influence scores in integrated items.

Sample PTE Speaking:

Read aloud: "Technology has changed the way we communicate" (say aloud clearly and naturally).

Sample PTE Writing:

Write an essay: "Some believe that the rise of artificial intelligence will create more problems than benefits. Discuss." (20 mins)

GMAT — quick facts

The GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test) is used for MBA and business-related graduate admissions. It measures Analytical Writing, Integrated Reasoning, Quantitative and Verbal skills. Adaptive scoring (computer-adaptive by question) is used for Quant and Verbal sections.

Format

  • Analytical Writing Assessment (AWA) — 30 minutes
  • Integrated Reasoning (IR) — 30 minutes
  • Quantitative — 62 minutes (31 Q)
  • Verbal — 65 minutes (36 Q)

Scoring

Total score ranges 200–800 (Quant + Verbal adaptive sections). AWA and IR have separate scores.

Prep plan (12 weeks)

  1. Weeks 1–2: Diagnostic and fundamentals (math basics and grammar)
  2. Weeks 3–6: Focused Quant practice and problem types
  3. Weeks 7–9: Verbal strategies — critical reasoning and sentence correction
  4. Weeks 10–11: Integrated Reasoning and AWA practice
  5. Week 12: Full-length adaptive practice tests and review

GMAT tips

  • For Quant, practice data sufficiency and problem solving thoroughly
  • In Verbal, focus on argument structure for critical reasoning
  • Time management is crucial — practice with adaptive logic in mind

Test-day

Bring valid ID, arrive early, and use breaks to reset focus. You may choose the order of sections on test day (select preferred order when booking).

Sample GMAT Quant (PS):

If x^2 − 5x + 6 = 0, what is the value of x? (Solve quickly.)

Sample GMAT CR:

A study shows a correlation between coffee consumption and alertness. Which assumption weakens the conclusion that coffee causes increased alertness?

GRE — quick facts

The GRE General Test is widely used for graduate admissions (non-MBA) and measures Verbal Reasoning, Quantitative Reasoning, and Analytical Writing. It can be taken at test centres or on some occasions at home.

Format

  • Analytical Writing — 1 issue + 1 argument (60 minutes total)
  • Verbal Reasoning — 2 sections, 30 minutes each
  • Quantitative Reasoning — 2 sections, 35 minutes each
  • Unscored/Research — may appear

Scoring

Verbal and Quant each scaled 130–170; AWA scored 0–6 in half-point increments.

Prep plan (10–12 weeks)

  1. Weeks 1–2: Diagnostic & basic math/reading review
  2. Weeks 3–6: Alternating Quant & Verbal intensive
  3. Weeks 7–9: Timed sections and essay practice
  4. Weeks 10–12: Full-length practice tests and refinement

GRE tips

  • For Verbal, learn common GRE vocabulary but focus on context use
  • Practice quantitative reasoning with pacing in mind
  • Write clear, well-structured essays for AWA — example templates help

Score use

Many programs report average GRE/GMAT scores of admitted students — check target programs for benchmarks.

Sample GRE Quant:

If 3x + 2 = 11, what is x?

Sample GRE Verbal (Text Completion):

Although the author is often __________, he displays unusual warmth in private, making him surprisingly __________.

Compare tests & choose the right one

Which test to pick depends on your goals:

  • Language for study/migration: IELTS or PTE — check institution or visa requirements.
  • Business school: GMAT (widely accepted) or GRE (increasingly accepted by many MBA programs).
  • Other graduate programs: GRE General is standard for many non-business graduate programs.

Quick decision guide

  1. Confirm required test with your target institution or visa authority.
  2. Compare format and timing with your test-taking strengths (computer vs paper, adaptive tests, speaking format).
  3. Estimate preparation time based on target scores and current level.

Registration, test-day checklist & resources

Registration

  • IELTS: book via British Council, IDP or local test centres.
  • PTE: register through Pearson PTE authorised centres.
  • GMAT: register at mba.com and choose centre/date; consider optional additional services.
  • GRE: register at ets.org and pick centre or at-home option where available.

Test-day checklist (general)

  • Bring the same valid ID you used to register (passport typically).
  • Arrive early — allow time for security and check-in.
  • Bring permitted stationery; for computer tests, you will be provided with materials.
  • Get a good night's sleep and eat a light, balanced meal before the test.

Top resources

  • Official sites: British Council/IDP (IELTS), Pearson (PTE), mba.com (GMAT), ETS (GRE)
  • Official practice materials and past papers
  • Cambridge and ETS prep books, Manhattan Prep, Magoosh, Khan Academy (quant basics)

Coaching vs self-study

Choose coaching if you need structured feedback (writing/speaking/essays). Self-study fits disciplined learners using official materials and mock tests.
