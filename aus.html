Courses & degree structure

Australia offers undergraduate (3–4 years), postgraduate taught (1–2 years), research degrees (MPhil/PhD), vocational education (TAFE), and short professional courses.

Undergraduate

Bachelor degrees typically run 3 years, or 4 for honours or professional programs (e.g., engineering, architecture).

Postgraduate

Master's by coursework usually 1–2 years; research master's and PhDs vary and often include scholarship opportunities.

Vocational & short courses

TAFE and private RTOs offer practical, job-focused training that can lead to employment or pathway into higher education.

Entry requirements

  • Academic transcripts and any required prerequisites
  • English language test (IELTS, TOEFL, PTE) or university exemptions
  • Personal statement or SOP for postgraduate applications
  • References for research degrees
  • Portfolio for creative courses

English test tip

Many universities provide conditional offers with required English modules or pathway programs if you need extra preparation.

Tuition fees & funding

Fees depend on level, university, and course — typically higher for STEM and professional degrees. Always check the official university fee pages for the latest figures.

Ballpark figures

  • Undergraduate (per year): AUD 20,000 — 45,000+
  • Postgraduate taught (per year): AUD 22,000 — 50,000+
  • Professional programs (e.g., medicine): AUD 40,000 — 80,000+

Scholarships & support

Look for university scholarships, research scholarships, and government-funded awards available to international students. Early applications improve your chances.

University scholarships

Merit-based and need-based awards directly from institutions.

Research scholarships

Often cover living stipend and tuition for research degrees.

Application process

Apply via each university's online portal or through authorised agents. For vocational (TAFE) courses, direct applications to institutes are common.

Documentation

  • Transcripts and translated documents if needed
  • Proof of English language ability
  • Statement of purpose and CV for postgraduate
  • Evidence of funds and health insurance where required

Interviews & offers

Some programs require interviews or skills checks. Offers may be unconditional or conditional (e.g., pending English test or final grades).

Student visa & basics

International students usually apply for an Australian student visa (subclass 500) tied to a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE). You must meet health, character, and financial requirements.

Key steps

  • Receive CoE from an approved education provider
  • Provide evidence of funds and meet health checks
  • Apply online for the student visa and provide biometrics if required
  • Purchase Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the duration of your stay

Work while studying

Student visas often permit part-time work (commonly 40 hours per fortnight during term). Check visa conditions for exact allowances.

Proof of funds (example)

You may need to show tuition plus living costs for the first year. Costs vary by city — capital cities are typically more expensive.

Health cover

OSHC protects you for basic medical care — arrange this before arrival and include it in your visa application if requested.

Living in Australia — costs & accommodation

Living costs depend on city, lifestyle, and housing choices. Major cities like Sydney and Melbourne are typically more expensive than regional locations.

Accommodation

  • University halls for first-year convenience
  • Shared rental apartments and homestays
  • Private rentals — require bond and references

Monthly budget (example)

  • Sydney & Melbourne: AUD 1,600 — 2,200
  • Other cities: AUD 1,200 — 1,700

Student life & support

Universities have student unions, clubs, and services for international students including orientation weeks, counselling, and career support.

Support services

  • International student advisors
  • Mental health & wellbeing services
  • Academic support & tutoring
  • Career and internship offices

Explore Australia

From beaches and rainforests to cities and outback, Australia has diverse landscapes to explore during breaks and weekends.

Work, internships & post-study pathways

Internships and industry experience boost employability. After graduating, eligible students may apply for post-study work visas depending on their course and other rules.

Post-study visas

Post-study work visas (Temporary Graduate visas) may be available to eligible graduates — durations vary by qualification and other factors.

Job search tips

  • Use university career services and job boards
  • Network with academics and industry partners
  • Create an Australian-style resume and LinkedIn profile

Practical tips for applicants

  • Start early — allow time for scholarships and visa processing
  • Tailor applications and SOPs to each course and supervisor
  • Confirm CRICOS codes and course duration before applying
  • Prepare authentic documentation for visa purposes
  • Consider regional campuses for cheaper living and work options

Frequently asked questions

How long is a master's degree in Australia?
Typically 1–2 years for taught master's; research degrees take longer depending on the field and project.
Can I work while studying?
Yes — student visas usually permit part-time work (check the exact hours allowed on your visa).
Do I need OSHC?
Yes — Overseas Student Health Cover is required for most student visa applicants to provide basic medical coverage.
Are there scholarships for international students?
Yes — universities and governments offer a range of scholarships and research stipends; check each university for details.
